Hiking Around the World

Whether it’s scaling alpine peaks or wandering desert canyons, hiking offers a deep connection with nature. Here are some of my favorite hikes and tips to get started.

Top Trails

  • Peru (Inca Trail): A historic hike to Machu Picchu.
  • Nepal (Annapurna Circuit): Himalayan vistas with cultural villages.
  • USA (John Muir Trail): A stunning traverse of the Sierra Nevada.
  • New Zealand (Routeburn Track): Lush rainforest to alpine ridges.

Recommended Gear

  • Lightweight hiking boots or trail runners
  • Backpack (30–50L for multi-day hikes)
  • Water filter or purification tablets
  • Trekking poles (especially for descents)
  • Quick-dry clothing and a good rain jacket

Travel Tips

  • Flights: Plan around shoulder seasons (spring/fall) for better weather and prices.
  • Accommodation: Many hikes offer refuges, teahouses, or require tents—check regulations.
  • Permits: Popular trails may need permits months in advance (e.g., Inca Trail).
  • Navigation: Download offline maps (Maps.me, Gaia GPS) before departure.
